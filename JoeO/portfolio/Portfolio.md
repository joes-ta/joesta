# Portfolio Suggestions
## Start with End in mind (show work in reverse)
## Have a result-oriented mindset. This is the way others will tend to look at it.
Think of résumé level, then drill down.
## Create a placeholder for WIP (Work In Progress), imagine the result
You'll want to start documenting projects before they are *complete*
## Make the portfolio development a nested result
1. Easieet system (start with what you know)
    1. Pencil and paper
    2. Google Docs
2. If you don't know `<Git and GitHUb>` make it a project
   * Learn Markdown
   * Learn GUI, then CLI
   * Learn init, clone
   * Learn add, commit, push, pull
   * Learn branches, etc.
## Insert projects as you go, Insert layers in projects as you go
* Development often starts with a vision, jumps to what you know, and fills in the gaps. Sometimes you find that you don't know enough and have to deepen your starting knowledge.
## Be as broad or focused as you like. You can always insert layers anywhere in your structure
* Me
  * Career 1
    * Education 1
      * Course 1
        *Project 1
  * Hobby 1
## You don't have to share each thing, 
but you may want share various things in the future. Structure information in ways that allow for levels to be extracted. Consider the overall purpose to be for yourself; realizing that yourself may desire to communicate with others.
